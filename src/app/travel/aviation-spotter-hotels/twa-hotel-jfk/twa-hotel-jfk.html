Quick facts

What spotters should know before booking

Best rooms

Ask for runway-view rooms in the Hughes Wing, higher floors (6+). North-facing rooms look straight onto 4L/22R and 4R/22L movements.

Rooftop pool deck

Open to hotel guests; great angle for 13L/31R and taxi shots. Bring a longer lens (200-400mm) for tight frames.

Light & time

Morning light favors 4L/4R arrivals, golden hour looks fantastic on departures from 31L/31R.

Audio & flight tracking

Plug into LiveATC (JFK tower/ground) and watch FR24/ADSBx to anticipate movements.

Shooting spots

Angles and what you can capture from each place

Hughes Wing rooms

Wide window frontage; tripod-friendly with curtains open. Heat haze can appear on hot summer days at mid-day—shoot mornings/evenings for crisp images.

Rooftop deck

Higher vantage for lineup and rotation shots. Mind pool reflections—polarizer helps. Security is used to guests with cameras; be respectful of other visitors.

Connie bar area

Great for atmosphere photos of the Lockheed Constellation itself. Not ideal for runway shots but a must-visit for night ambience.
